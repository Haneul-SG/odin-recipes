Bulgogi

All About This Delicious Korean Staple Dish

Bulgogi,pronounced as bool-goh-giis literally "fire meat", is a gui (구이; Korean-style grilled or roasted dish) made of thin, marinated slices of meat, most commonly beef, grilled on a barbecue or on a stove-top griddle. It is also often stir-fried in a pan in home cooking. Sirloin, rib eye or brisket are frequently used cuts of beef for the dish.

The dish originated from northern areas of the Korean Peninsula, but is a very popular dish in South Korea, where it can be found anywhere from upscale restaurants to local supermarkets as pan-ready kits.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 Pounds of boneless ribeye steak
  • 1/2 Small peeled and grated pear
  • 1/4 Cup of reduced sodium soy sauce
  • 2 Tablespoons of brown sugar
  • 2 Tablespoons of toasted sesame oil
  • 3 Cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 Tablespoon of freshly grated ginger
  • 1 Tablespoon of Korean Red Pepper Paste
  • 2 Tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 Thinly sliced green onions
  • 1 Teaspoon of toasted sesame seeds

Directions

Preparing the Meat for Marination

  1. Wrap steak in plastic wrap.
  2. Place the steak in the freezer for 30 minutes.
  3. Unwrap and slice across the grain into 1/4-inch thick slices.

Marinating the Meat

  1. In a medium bowl, combine pear, soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger and gochujang.
  2. In a gallon size Ziploc bag, combine soy sauce mixture and steak.
  3. Allow the meat to marinate for at least 2 hours to overnight, turning the bag occasionally.

Cooking the Meat

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon vegetable oil in a cast iron grill pan over medium-high heat.
  2. In batches, add steak to the grill pan in a single layer and cook, flipping once, until charred and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  3. Repeat with remaining 1 tablespoon vegetable oil and steak.
  4. Remove the steak from the pan.
  5. Serve immediately, garnished with green onions and sesame seeds, if desired.
